National Anthem

Composed by Rabindranath Tagore

Jana Gana Mana (Hindi)

जन-गण-मन अधिनायक जय हे,
भारत-भाग्य-विधाता।
पंजाब सिन्ध गुजरात मराठा,
द्राविड़ उत्कल बंग।
विंध्य हिमाचल यमुना गंगा,
उच्छल जलधि तरंग।
तव शुभ नामे जागे,
तव शुभ आशिष मांगे,
गाहे तव जय गाथा।
जन-गण-मंगलदायक जय हे,
भारत-भाग्य-विधाता।
जय हे, जय हे, जय हे,
जय जय जय जय हे॥

About the National Anthem

The National Anthem of India "Jana Gana Mana", composed originally in Bengali by Rabindranath Tagore, was adopted in its Hindi version by the Constituent Assembly as the National Anthem of India on 24 January 1950. It was first sung on 27 December 1911 at the Calcutta Session of the Indian National Congress.

Playing time: Approximately 52 seconds.

National Song

Composed by Bankim Chandra Chatterjee

Vande Mataram — Full Song (Sanskrit)

वन्दे मातरम्।
सुजलां सुफलां मलयजशीतलाम्,
शस्यश्यामलां मातरम्।
शुभ्रज्योत्स्नापुलकितयामिनीम्,
फुल्लकुसुमितद्रुमदलशोभिनीम्,
सुहासिनीं सुमधुर भाषिणीम्,
सुखदां वरदां मातरम्॥

कोटि कोटि कण्ठ कल कल निनाद कराले,
कोटि कोटि भुजैर्धृत खरकरवाले,
अबला केन मा एत बले।
बहुबलधारिणीं नमामि तारिणीम्,
रिपुदलवारिणीं मातरम्॥

तुमि विद्या तुमि धर्म,
तुमि हृदि तुमि मर्म,
त्वं हि प्राणाः शरीरे।
बाहुते तुमि मा शक्ति,
हृदये तुमि मा भक्ति,
तोमारइ प्रतिमा गडि मन्दिरे मन्दिरे॥

त्वं हि दुर्गा दशप्रहरणधारिणी,
कमला कमलदलविहारिणी,
वाणी विद्यादायिनी,
नमामि त्वाम्,
नमामि कमलाम्,
अमलां अतुलाम्,
सुजलां सुफलां मातरम्॥

श्यामलां सरलां सुस्मिताम् भूषिताम्,
धरणीं भरणीं मातरम्॥
वन्दे मातरम्।

About the National Song

The song "Vande Mataram", composed in Sanskrit by Bankim Chandra Chatterjee, was a source of inspiration to the people in their struggle for freedom. It has an equal status with Jana Gana Mana. The first political occasion when it was sung was the 1896 session of the Indian National Congress.

It was originally part of Bankim Chandra's novel "Anandamath" published in 1882. The song played a vital role in the Indian independence movement and was first sung in a political context at the 1896 session of the Indian National Congress.

On 24 January 1950, President Dr. Rajendra Prasad stated in the Constituent Assembly that "the song Vande Mataram, which has played a historic part in the struggle for Indian freedom, shall be honoured equally with Jana Gana Mana and shall have equal status with it."

English Translation (First Stanza)

"I bow to thee, Mother,
richly-watered, richly-fruited,
cool with the winds of the south,
dark with the crops of the harvests,
the Mother!
Her nights rejoicing in the glory of the moonlight,
her lands clothed beautifully with her trees in flowering bloom,
sweet of laughter, sweet of speech,
the Mother, giver of boons, giver of bliss."

Other National Symbols of India

Symbols that represent India's rich heritage, culture, and natural diversity

National Flag

Tiranga (Tricolour)

The National Flag of India is a horizontal tricolour of deep saffron (courage), white (truth & peace) with the Ashoka Chakra (wheel of law with 24 spokes) in navy blue at the centre, and India green (fertility & prosperity) at the bottom. Adopted on 22 July 1947, the flag was designed by Pingali Venkayya.

National Emblem

Lion Capital of Ashoka

The State Emblem of India is an adaptation of the Lion Capital of Ashoka at Sarnath. It features four Asiatic lions standing back to back, symbolising power, courage, pride, and confidence. The motto "Satyameva Jayate" (Truth Alone Triumphs) is inscribed below. Adopted on 26 January 1950.

National Animal

Royal Bengal Tiger (Panthera tigris)

The magnificent Royal Bengal Tiger is India's national animal, symbolising grace, strength, agility, and power. India is home to about 70% of the world's tiger population. Project Tiger was launched in 1973 to protect this majestic animal from extinction.

National Bird

Indian Peacock (Pavo cristatus)

The Indian Peacock, with its resplendent plumage of iridescent blue and green feathers, was declared the national bird of India in 1963. It symbolises grace, joy, beauty, and pride. The peacock is deeply woven into Indian mythology, art, and culture.

National Flower

Lotus (Nelumbo nucifera)

The Lotus is the national flower of India, symbolising divinity, fertility, wealth, knowledge, and purity. It holds a sacred place in Indian culture and mythology. The lotus blooms beautifully even in muddy waters, representing triumph of the spirit over adversity.

National Fruit

Mango (Mangifera indica)

The Mango, known as the "King of Fruits", has been cultivated in India for over 5,000 years. It symbolises prosperity and fertility. India is the world's largest producer of mangoes, with over 100 varieties grown across the country, including the famous Alphonso, Dasheri, and Langra.

National Tree

Indian Banyan (Ficus benghalensis)

The Indian Banyan tree represents eternal life due to its seemingly ever-expanding branches. It is often the focal point of village life ("Panchayat tree"). The Great Banyan in Kolkata's Botanical Garden is over 250 years old and covers approximately 14,500 sq. metres.

National River

Ganga (Ganges)

The Ganga is the longest river of India, flowing 2,525 km from the Gangotri glacier in the Himalayas to the Bay of Bengal. Revered as "Ganga Maa" (Mother Ganga), it is the lifeline for millions of people, supporting agriculture, commerce, and spiritual practices across the Indo-Gangetic plain.

National Aquatic Animal

Gangetic River Dolphin (Platanista gangetica)

The Gangetic River Dolphin was declared the national aquatic animal of India in 2009. This nearly blind freshwater dolphin navigates using ultrasonic echolocation. It is an indicator of the health of the river ecosystem and is classified as endangered, with fewer than 2,000 remaining.

National Heritage Animal

Indian Elephant (Elephas maximus indicus)

The Indian Elephant was declared the National Heritage Animal in 2010 by the Government of India. Elephants have been an integral part of Indian culture, religion, and history for millennia. India is home to about 60% of the Asian elephant population, primarily in the Western Ghats and northeastern states.

National Reptile

King Cobra (Ophiophagus hannah)

The King Cobra, the world's longest venomous snake, is the national reptile of India. It can grow up to 18 feet long and is revered in Indian mythology, often depicted with Lord Shiva. Found in the rainforests and plains of India, it holds a significant place in Indian folklore and culture.

National Currency

Indian Rupee (₹ / INR)

The Indian Rupee (₹) is the official currency of India, issued by the Reserve Bank of India. The symbol ₹ was adopted on 15 July 2010, designed by D. Udaya Kumar. It is derived from the Devanagari consonant "र" (Ra) and the Latin letter "R", representing India's international identity.
