History of India's Independence

The British Raj and the Struggle for Freedom

The British East India Company arrived in India in the early 1600s as traders, but by the mid-19th century, they had taken control of most of the subcontinent. The first major uprising against their rule was the Rebellion of 1857.

Following this, the British Crown took direct control, establishing the British Raj. The late 19th and early 20th centuries saw the rise of a unified nationalist movement, spearheaded by the Indian National Congress.

The Gandhian Era

Mahatma Gandhi's return to India in 1915 transformed the independence movement into a mass struggle. Through nonviolent civil disobedience campaigns like the Non-Cooperation Movement, the Salt March (Dandi March), and the Quit India Movement, millions of ordinary Indians joined the fight.

Partition and Independence

After decades of struggle and the weakening of the British Empire following World War II, the British finally agreed to leave India. However, this came at the cost of partitioning the country into India and Pakistan.

On August 15, 1947, India awoke to light and freedom as its first Prime Minister, Jawaharlal Nehru, delivered his famous "Tryst with Destiny" speech.
